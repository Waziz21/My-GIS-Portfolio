    Complete Village Mapping - Pesawaran Regency

    Between June and December 2023, I participated as a private consultant in a large-scale cadastral mapping project covering 15 villages across Pesawaran Regency, as part of the Ministry of Agrarian Affairs and Spatial Planning/National Land Agency’s Reforma Agraria program. This initiative supported Indonesia’s One Map Policy by mapping approximately 5,000–7,000 land parcels spanning 10,100 hectares. Utilizing UAV photogrammetry and GNSS RTK surveys, our team first captured aerial imagery, then reconciled it with existing registered parcel data. For unregistered parcels, we conducted field surveys and digitized each plot to ensure spatial accuracy and completeness. The final outputs included comprehensive parcel maps, shapefiles, and textual ownership records, contributing to legal land registration and national spatial data integration.

    Land Acquisition for Margatiga Dam Project

    From April to June 2021, I was involved in a land acquisition project in Margatiga District, East Lampung Regency, supporting the construction of the Margatiga Dam under the Ministry of Agrarian Affairs and Spatial Planning/National Land Agency. The dam is intended to supply irrigation water to over 16,000 hectares of agricultural land. Our work focused on mapping and verifying 6,340 land parcels covering a total area of 2,213.8 hectares. Using GNSS RTK technology alongside cadastral document matching, we ensured accurate spatial data collection. In addition to mapping, we assessed physical land assets—including buildings, pools, well depths, and planted vegetation—to provide a basis for compensation valuation. The final deliverables included detailed parcel maps and supporting data essential for the compensation process and successful dam development.

    Complete Village Mapping - East Lampung

    In March–April 2021, a thematic land and spatial mapping project was carried out in Margo Toto Village, East Lampung Regency, as part of the Indonesian Ministry of Agrarian Affairs and Spatial Planning/National Land Agency’s ongoing cadastral initiatives. The project focused on identifying and mapping approximately 800–1,000 land parcels within the village using GNSS technology and app-based data recording tools. The main objective was to support spatial data integration and improve land tenure clarity. The final deliverables included parcel shapefiles and detailed tabular data containing land parcel identification and ownership information.
